1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how SunMatch ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website (the "Service").

SunMatch operates as an independent platform that helps homeowners connect with and compare solar service providers. We do not provide solar services directly.

By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly and provide information about user behavior and interactions.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies: expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: remain on your device until deleted or expired
  • First-party cookies: set by our website
  • Third-party cookies: set by external services

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are required for the website to function properly.

They support:

  • security
  • session management
  • accessibility features

Examples include:

  • remembering cookie preferences
  • maintaining active sessions

3.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with our website.

They are used to:

  • analyze traffic
  • improve performance
  • identify usability issues

Example:

  • Google Analytics

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies enhance user experience by remembering preferences.

They may store:

  • language settings
  • form inputs
  • interface preferences

3.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies are used to:

  • track visitor behavior across websites
  • measure advertising effectiveness
  • display relevant ads

They may be set by advertising partners or social media platforms.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services such as:

  • analytics providers
  • advertising networks
  • social media platforms

These third parties may place cookies on your device and collect data about your browsing activity.

We do not control these cookies. Please review their respective privacy policies.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject cookies.

Most browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to disable them.

5.1 Browser Settings

You can manage cookies through your browser settings:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You may opt out of certain tracking technologies using tools such as:

  • Google Analytics Opt-Out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance
  • Network Advertising Initiative

Note: Disabling cookies may affect website functionality and user experience.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature.

Currently, there is no universal standard for responding to DNT signals, and our website does not respond to them.
